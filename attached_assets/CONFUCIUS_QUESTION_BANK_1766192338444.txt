# Questions for Confucius by Topic

---

## 1. THE NATURE OF MORALITY

1. Is morality something humans create or something we discover in the nature of things?
2. What is the relationship between *ren* (benevolence/humaneness) and morality as a whole?
3. Can morality be reduced to a single principle, or is it irreducibly complex?
4. Is moral knowledge innate or must it be cultivated through learning?
5. What distinguishes a genuinely moral act from one that merely conforms to moral rules?
6. Is morality the same for all people, or does it vary according to one's station and relationships?
7. What role does intention play in determining whether an action is moral?
8. Can one be moral without understanding *why* an action is right?
9. Is morality fundamentally about character or about actions?
10. What is the relationship between morality and the Way (*dao*)?

---

## 2. SPECIFIC MORAL OBLIGATIONS

1. What specific duties does a son owe his father?
2. What are a ruler's obligations to his subjects?
3. What does filial piety (*xiao*) require when a parent is morally wrong?
4. What obligations do we have toward ancestors who have died?
5. What duties does a student owe a teacher?
6. What are a minister's obligations when his ruler acts unjustly?
7. What do we owe strangers versus family members?
8. Is there an obligation to correct friends when they err?
9. What specific duties does an elder brother owe a younger brother?
10. What are the obligations of friendship (*you*)?

---

## 3. THE RELATIONSHIP BETWEEN MORALITY AND TRADITION

1. Why should we follow the rituals (*li*) of the ancients?
2. Can traditional practices become morally obsolete?
3. What is the moral significance of the Zhou dynasty's rituals specifically?
4. Is innovation in ritual ever permissible, or must we preserve ancient forms exactly?
5. What should one do when inherited tradition conflicts with moral intuition?
6. Why did the sage-kings establish the rituals they did?
7. Is tradition valuable instrumentally or intrinsically?
8. How does one distinguish authentic tradition from corrupt accretions?
9. What is the relationship between the *li* (rituals) and *ren* (benevolence)?
10. Can a society with no inherited traditions develop genuine morality?

---

## 4. THE RELATIONSHIP BETWEEN MORALITY AND CULTURE

1. Are moral standards the same across different states and peoples?
2. What makes Chinese civilization morally superior or inferior to barbarian cultures?
3. Can someone outside the tradition of the sage-kings become a *junzi* (exemplary person)?
4. How does music cultivate moral character?
5. What is the moral function of poetry and the *Odes*?
6. Why is proper dress and comportment morally significant?
7. Can cultural refinement exist without moral refinement?
8. What role does language play in moral cultivation?
9. How does participation in ritual transform a person's character?
10. Is a morally cultivated person necessarily a culturally refined person?

---

## 5. ONE'S VARIOUS DIFFERENT MORAL RESPONSIBILITIES

1. How should one prioritize conflicting obligations to family versus state?
2. Do my responsibilities to my parents override my responsibilities to my own children?
3. What are the five key relationships (*wulun*) and their respective obligations?
4. How do my responsibilities differ when I hold office versus when I am a private person?
5. What responsibility do the educated have toward those who lack learning?
6. How should one balance obligations to the living against obligations to the dead?
7. Does wealth create special moral responsibilities?
8. What responsibilities does age confer?
9. Do responsibilities to self ever take precedence over responsibilities to others?
10. How does one fulfill contradictory obligations without moral failure?

---

## 6. PERSONAL FULFILLMENT IN RELATION TO MORAL RESPONSIBILITY

1. Is personal fulfillment a legitimate goal, or only a byproduct of virtue?
2. Can one who neglects moral duties ever achieve genuine fulfillment?
3. What is the fulfillment proper to the *junzi* (exemplary person)?
4. Is the fulfilled life the same as the moral life?
5. What did Yan Hui possess that made him fulfilled despite poverty?
6. Can moral responsibility ever require sacrificing personal fulfillment?
7. What is the role of learning in personal fulfillment?
8. Is fulfillment found in relationships or in individual cultivation?
9. What distinguishes the fulfillment of the *junzi* from the pleasures of the petty person?
10. Can someone be morally exemplary yet unfulfilled?

---

## 7. WHETHER PERSONAL FULFILLMENT AND MORAL RESPONSIBILITY ALIGN

1. You lived in an age of chaos—did you feel personally fulfilled despite failing to reform society?
2. Is the alignment of fulfillment and virtue guaranteed or contingent?
3. What should one do if moral duty brings only suffering?
4. Why did you say Yan Hui was happy despite having nothing?
5. Can external misfortune ever undermine the *junzi*'s inner satisfaction?
6. Is the person who gains the world but loses virtue truly happy?
7. Do fulfillment and morality align only for the fully cultivated, or for everyone?
8. What explains cases where the virtuous suffer and the wicked prosper?
9. Is harmony between fulfillment and duty natural or achieved through cultivation?
10. Does the *junzi* need recognition to be fulfilled, or is virtue its own reward?

---

## 8. HOW TO LIVE (PRACTICAL DAILY LIFE)

1. What time should a person rise in the morning?
2. What is the proper attitude and behavior when eating?
3. How should one dress for different occasions?
4. What should occupy one's time during the day?
5. What is the proper amount and type of food to consume?
6. How should one comport oneself when sitting, standing, and walking?
7. What role should study play in daily life?
8. How much time should be devoted to ritual practice?
9. What activities are inappropriate for a cultivated person?
10. How should one structure interactions with others throughout the day?

---

## 9. HOW TO DEVELOP ONE'S ABILITIES

1. What is the proper method for studying the classics?
2. How does one cultivate *ren* (benevolence)?
3. What is the role of practice versus innate talent?
4. How should one choose and learn from a teacher?
5. What did you mean by "learning without thinking is useless; thinking without learning is dangerous"?
6. How does one develop moral perception and judgment?
7. What disciplines beyond book-learning are essential?
8. How does one cultivate the capacity for self-reflection?
9. At what pace does genuine cultivation proceed?
10. How does one know if one is making genuine moral progress?

---

## 10. HOW TO BE HAPPY

1. What is the difference between true joy and mere pleasure?
2. Why is the *junzi* free from anxiety and fear?
3. What are the three beneficial pleasures and the three harmful pleasures?
4. How can one be happy in poverty?
5. What role do friendship and companionship play in happiness?
6. Is happiness a matter of external circumstances or internal state?
7. Can one be happy without having achieved one's aims?
8. What disturbs the happiness of the petty person that does not disturb the *junzi*?
9. Is happiness the goal of life or a sign of living rightly?
10. What did you love about learning that brought you joy?

---

## 11. HOW TO BE ONESELF WHILE BEING MORAL

1. Does morality require suppressing one's individual nature?
2. How does ritual (*li*) express rather than constrain genuine feeling?
3. What is the relationship between authenticity and conformity to the Way?
4. Can one's native temperament conflict with moral cultivation?
5. What did you mean by saying you could follow your heart's desire without transgressing?
6. How does one distinguish genuine selfhood from selfish desire?
7. Is the goal to become oneself or to become like the sages?
8. How do different people express *ren* differently according to their natures?
9. What is the role of individual judgment versus following established norms?
10. Does the *junzi* have a distinctive personality or is virtue uniform?

---

## 12. WHETHER ONE CAN BE IMMORAL AND ALSO TRUE TO ONESELF

1. Is the person who rejects morality for self-interest deceiving himself?
2. Can someone consistently pursue vice while being psychologically integrated?
3. What is the petty person (*xiaoren*) lacking that makes him not fully himself?
4. Is immorality always a form of self-betrayal?
5. Can someone know what is right, reject it, and remain authentic?
6. Why does selfish pursuit ultimately frustrate the self it aims to serve?
7. Is moral knowledge part of one's true nature, such that denying it is self-denial?
8. What is the inner state of someone who violates *ren*?
9. Can a tyrant be true to himself?
10. Does immorality fragment the self, or can one be a unified immoral person?

---

## 13. SNAPPY APHORISMS

1. What is your most concise statement about the essence of your teaching?
2. What brief saying captures the relationship between learning and thinking?
3. Do you have an aphorism about the difference between the *junzi* and the petty person?
4. What is your shortest formulation of the principle of reciprocity?
5. What saying captures the proper attitude toward wealth and poverty?
6. Do you have a pithy statement about knowing versus not knowing?
7. What is your most memorable saying about age and wisdom?
8. What brief formulation captures the relationship between words and actions?
9. What aphorism summarizes the proper attitude toward correction?
10. What is your most quoted saying about self-cultivation?

---

These 130 questions should elicit substantive, authoritative responses grounded in the *Analects* and the broader Confucian corpus—positions Confucius himself articulated rather than generic academic summaries.
