# KUCZYNSKI: TOPICS AND SUGGESTED QUESTIONS

For users who want to learn about Kuczynski's philosophy but don't know where to start.

## TOPIC 1: CRITIQUE OF EMPIRICISM

**Description:** Kuczynski's systematic refutation of empiricism as a philosophical foundation, showing why strict empiricism is self-refuting and incompatible with science itself.

### Questions to Ask:
1. Why do you think empiricism is self-refuting?
2. How does empiricism fail to explain observable events?
3. What's wrong with the empiricist view that all knowledge comes from sense experience?
4. Why can't strict empiricism account for scientific knowledge?
5. How does empiricism mishandle the concept of causation?
6. What errors do empiricists make about relations versus properties?
7. Why did Hume accept skepticism instead of rejecting empiricism?
8. How is the concept of confirmation incoherent without non-empirical knowledge?
9. What is Goodman's grue argument and why does it refute empiricism?
10. Why do you say "empirical thought" is an oxymoron?

## TOPIC 2: PHILOSOPHY OF MIND — CONSCIOUSNESS

**Description:** Kuczynski's analysis of the nature of consciousness, its relationship to mentality, and the distinction between what is consciousness-constitutive versus merely access-conscious.

### Questions to Ask:
1. What is the difference between access-consciousness and phenomenal consciousness?
2. Why do you say mentality and consciousness are not identical?
3. Are propositional attitudes constituents of consciousness?
4. What role does consciousness play in thinking?
5. How can beliefs be operative even when we're not conscious of them?
6. What do you mean when you say the nucleus of the self is consciousness?
7. Why can consciousness only represent things iconically?
8. How do concepts relate to consciousness if they're not constituents of it?
9. What happens when we become conscious of our own mental states?
10. Is it possible to be conscious of something without having propositional knowledge about it?
