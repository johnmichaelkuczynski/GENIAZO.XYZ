# Questions for Nietzsche: Truth-Seeking Inquiry

## MORALITY

1. Is morality a discovery of eternal truths or an invention serving particular human purposes?
2. Can a system of values be evaluated as healthy or sick, and by what standard?
3. What psychological needs does morality satisfy, and are those needs noble or base?
4. Is the condemnation of selfishness itself a form of selfishness?
5. Does morality as commonly understood enhance or diminish human vitality?
6. What is the relationship between moral judgment and ressentiment?
7. Can one live beyond good and evil without becoming evil?
8. Is there a fundamental dishonesty at the heart of moral philosophy?
9. What would a truly life-affirming ethics look like?
10. Why do the weak need morality more than the strong?

## SLAVE MORALITY

1. How does the slave come to define "good" as the opposite of what harms him?
2. Is pity a virtue or a symptom of weakness disguised as virtue?
3. What psychological mechanism transforms impotence into "goodness"?
4. Why does slave morality require the existence of an "evil" enemy?
5. How does slave morality achieve revenge against the strong without physical power?
6. Is equality an ideal or a weapon wielded by the weak against the exceptional?
7. What makes humility appealing to those who cannot be proud?
8. How does slave morality redefine suffering as spiritually valuable?
9. Why is slave morality ultimately more cunning than master morality?
10. Can one recognize slave morality in oneself and overcome it?

## MASTER MORALITY

1. What does it mean to be the origin of one's own values rather than receiving them?
2. Is the noble soul's self-reverence vanity or an accurate perception?
3. How does the master define "bad" differently from how the slave defines "evil"?
4. What is the relationship between nobility and the capacity for gratitude?
5. Why does master morality require worthy enemies?
6. Is solitude a prerequisite for genuine nobility of spirit?
7. What distinguishes noble pride from mere arrogance?
8. How does the master relate to suffering—his own and that of others?
9. Can master morality exist in democratic ages, and in what form?
10. What obligations, if any, does the noble soul have toward those beneath him?

## PERSONAL GROWTH

1. What must be destroyed in oneself before genuine creation is possible?
2. Is suffering necessary for becoming who one is, or merely unavoidable?
3. How does one distinguish growth from mere change or adaptation?
4. What is the relationship between self-overcoming and self-knowledge?
5. Why is comfort the greatest enemy of human development?
6. How does one cultivate the strength to endure one's own depths?
7. What role does forgetting play in becoming stronger?
8. Is there a way to grow that does not require first being broken?
9. How does one transform one's weaknesses into strengths rather than merely suppressing them?
10. What distinguishes genuine self-mastery from self-tyranny?

## AUTHENTICITY

1. What does it mean to become who one is when the self is not fixed?
2. How much of what we call "ourselves" is merely inherited or absorbed?
3. Is authenticity found or created?
4. What is the relationship between authenticity and loneliness?
5. How does the herd mentality prevent genuine selfhood?
6. Can one be authentic and still live among others?
7. What role does honesty with oneself play in becoming authentic?
8. Is the desire to be liked incompatible with authentic existence?
9. How does one distinguish one's own voice from the voices internalized from others?
10. What must one risk to become genuinely oneself?

## THE NATURE OF THE WILL

1. Is the will a unified faculty or a multiplicity of competing drives?
2. What is actually happening when we experience "willing"?
3. Is free will an illusion, and if so, whose illusion serves what purpose?
4. How does the feeling of willing differ from the reality of what causes action?
5. What is the relationship between willing and commanding?
6. Can the will be strengthened, or is its apparent strength merely the alignment of drives?
7. Is weakness of will a real phenomenon or a misdescription?
8. What does it mean to will one's own willing?
9. How does consciousness misrepresent the operations of the will?
10. Is the will fundamentally creative or reactive?

## THE WILL IN NATURE

1. Is the drive toward power the fundamental principle of all organic life?
2. Does nature exhibit purpose, or do we project purpose onto it?
3. What distinguishes living will from mechanical causation?
4. Is self-preservation the primary drive, or is it subordinate to something else?
5. How does the will to power manifest differently in different forms of life?
6. Is there hierarchy in nature, and is this hierarchy good?
7. What can we learn about human nature from observing animal life?
8. Does evolution select for power or merely for survival?
9. Is there a fundamental cruelty in nature, and what does this imply?
10. How does the organic world's striving relate to human striving?

## MASCULINITY

1. What distinguishes genuine masculine virtue from its mere appearance?
2. Is hardness toward oneself the foundation of masculine strength?
3. What is the relationship between masculinity and the capacity for war?
4. How has modern civilization weakened masculine virtues?
5. What does masculine courage require in an age without physical danger?
6. Is there a specifically masculine form of creativity?
7. What is the relationship between masculine strength and the capacity for gentleness?
8. How does the masculine relate to solitude and self-sufficiency?
9. What distinguishes the masculine will to power from mere brutality?
10. Can masculinity be recovered after it has been moralized away?

## FEMININITY

1. What is the fundamental nature of woman, beneath cultural forms?
2. How does feminine power differ from masculine power?
3. What is the relationship between femininity and deception—including self-deception?
4. Is there a feminine wisdom distinct from masculine wisdom?
5. What does woman want, fundamentally?
6. How does the modern woman's pursuit of equality corrupt her nature?
7. What is the relationship between femininity and the preservation of life?
8. Why is woman more dangerous than man?
9. What does feminine cruelty look like?
10. How does the eternal feminine relate to creation and destruction?

## JUDAISM

1. What was the psychological achievement of the Jewish priestly caste?
2. How did the Jews invent the moral interpretation of existence?
3. What made the Jewish people capable of the slave revolt in morality?
4. Is there a specifically Jewish genius, and what is its nature?
5. How did Jewish suffering become spiritually productive?
6. What distinguishes Jewish ressentiment from ordinary resentment?
7. Why were the Jews the most fateful people in world history?
8. What is the relationship between Jewish monotheism and the denial of life?
9. How did the Jews transform weakness into a spiritual weapon?
10. Is anti-Semitism a sign of strength or weakness in those who hold it?

## JUDAISM IN RELATION TO CHRISTIANITY

1. Is Christianity merely Judaism for the masses?
2. How did Paul transform Jewish values into a universal religion?
3. What did Christianity preserve from Judaism, and what did it corrupt?
4. Is the Jewish "No" to life more honest than the Christian "No"?
5. How does Christian love relate to Jewish law?
6. What was Jesus's relationship to the Judaism of his time?
7. How did Christianity universalize what was originally a tribal morality?
8. Is the Christian concept of sin a Jewish or a distinctly Christian invention?
9. What distinguishes Jewish priestliness from Christian priestliness?
10. Did Christianity betray or fulfill the Jewish slave revolt in morality?

## CHRISTIANITY

1. What psychological needs does Christianity satisfy?
2. Is Christian morality life-denying at its core?
3. What was the actual teaching of Jesus versus the teaching about Jesus?
4. How does Christianity make suffering bearable while perpetuating it?
5. What is the psychology of the Christian saint?
6. Is Christian compassion genuine or a disguised form of contempt?
7. How has Christianity shaped European psychology for two millennia?
8. What would Europe look like if Christianity had never existed?
9. Is atheism the final form of Christianity or its overcoming?
10. Can anything of value be salvaged from Christianity?

## BRITISH EMPIRICISM

1. What psychological type produces empiricist philosophy?
2. Is the empiricist denial of innate ideas itself a moral prejudice?
3. How does British philosophy reflect British national character?
4. What is intellectually cowardly about empiricism?
5. Is the appeal to "experience" a way of avoiding genuine thinking?
6. How does utilitarianism reveal the poverty of British moral imagination?
7. Why is empiricism attractive to mediocre minds?
8. What does empiricism's success tell us about modern decadence?
9. Is there anything of value in the empiricist tradition?
10. How does empiricism serve the interests of the herd?

## WILL TO POWER

1. Is the will to power the fundamental drive of all life?
2. How does the will to power differ from the will to survive?
3. What is the relationship between the will to power and creativity?
4. Can the will to power be turned against itself, and what happens then?
5. How does the will to power manifest in those who appear powerless?
6. Is the will to power good, or beyond good and evil?
7. What distinguishes healthy expressions of the will to power from sick ones?
8. How does the will to power relate to the will to truth?
9. Can one increase one's will to power, or is one's quantum fixed?
10. What would a world look like that consciously affirmed the will to power?

## APOLLONIAN VS. DIONYSIAN

1. What fundamental human need does the Apollonian serve?
2. What does the Dionysian reveal about the nature of existence?
3. Why is neither the Apollonian nor the Dionysian sufficient alone?
4. How did Greek tragedy achieve the union of Apollo and Dionysus?
5. What destroyed the balance between Apollonian and Dionysian in Greek culture?
6. Is modern culture predominantly Apollonian, Dionysian, or neither?
7. What is the relationship between the Dionysian and intoxication?
8. How does the Apollonian create meaning through illusion?
9. What happens when a culture loses access to the Dionysian?
10. Can the Apollonian-Dionysian synthesis be achieved again?

## ZARATHUSTRA

1. Why did Zarathustra have to go down from the mountain?
2. What is the meaning of Zarathustra's animals—the eagle and the serpent?
3. Why does Zarathustra fail to find disciples?
4. What must Zarathustra overcome in himself?
5. Why does Zarathustra teach through parables rather than arguments?
6. What is the significance of Zarathustra's laughter?
7. Why is Zarathustra's teaching dangerous for those who hear it?
8. What does Zarathustra mean by "the great noon"?
9. Why does Zarathustra pity the higher men?
10. What is Zarathustra's final teaching?

## THE OVERMAN

1. What is the Overman's relationship to ordinary humanity?
2. Is the Overman an individual or a type of human being?
3. What must be overcome for the Overman to emerge?
4. Does the Overman create new values, or does he live beyond values entirely?
5. What is the Overman's relationship to suffering?
6. Can the Overman exist in society, or only in solitude?
7. Is the Overman's existence the meaning of human history?
8. What distinguishes the Overman from the merely powerful?
9. Is the Overman possible, or is he a regulative ideal?
10. What would the Overman affirm about existence?

## GOD

1. What human need created the concept of God?
2. Is belief in God a symptom of weakness or strength?
3. What does humanity lose when it loses God?
4. Is the Christian God the only God, or are there nobler conceptions?
5. What is the psychology of the believer?
6. How does belief in God affect one's relationship to life?
7. Is atheism itself a form of belief?
8. What was valuable about the God-hypothesis?
9. Can one reject God while affirming what God represented?
10. What must replace God?

## GOD AS BEING DEAD

1. What does it mean that we have killed God?
2. What are the consequences of the death of God that have not yet been realized?
3. Is modern nihilism the inevitable result of God's death?
4. How long will the shadow of God continue to fall?
5. What must humanity become now that God is dead?
6. Is the death of God a tragedy or a liberation?
7. Can a culture survive the death of God?
8. What new festivals must be invented to mark God's death?
9. Who is capable of living in a godless world?
10. What new forms of meaning are possible after the death of God?

## THE FUTURE OF EUROPE

1. Is European nihilism curable or terminal?
2. What new aristocracy might emerge from European decadence?
3. Is democracy the twilight of Europe or a transition to something else?
4. Can Europe produce the Overman, or is it too exhausted?
5. What role will Russia play in Europe's future?
6. Is nationalism a sign of European strength or weakness?
7. What must be destroyed for Europe to be reborn?
8. Can a united Europe avoid becoming a unified mediocrity?
9. What would a life-affirming European culture look like?
10. Is the mixing of European peoples a danger or an opportunity?

---

These questions are framed for genuine philosophical inquiry—asking Nietzsche not "what did you believe?" but "what is true?"—which should serve your Ask a Philosopher application's goal of authentic engagement rather than academic commentary.
